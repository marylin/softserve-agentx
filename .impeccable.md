## Design Context

### Users
SRE engineers and on-call developers working at e-commerce companies running Medusa.js. They use this tool during incident response -- often under time pressure, in dark office environments, sometimes late at night. The job: submit an incident report and get an AI-triaged, severity-classified, routed ticket with minimal friction. They also use the dashboard to monitor open incidents, component health, and pipeline metrics.

### Brand Personality
Calm, confident, precise. The interface should feel like a trusted colleague who already did the work -- not an alarm system screaming for attention. Information is presented with clarity and hierarchy so the SRE can make decisions fast without cognitive overload.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Linear + Notion hybrid. Clean, minimal dark UI with structured content areas. Refined rather than flashy. Typography-driven hierarchy over decorative elements.
- **Theme:** Dark mode primary (SREs work in dark environments, often on-call at night).
- **Accent color:** Blue/indigo (calming, reliable, Linear-adjacent). Shift from current orange.
- **Anti-references:** PagerDuty (too noisy/urgent), Grafana (too dense/overwhelming), generic admin templates (card grids, glassmorphism, gradient text).
- **References:** Linear (clean issue tracker), Notion (structured content, warm dark theme), Vercel dashboard (sharp typography, minimal chrome).

### Design Principles
1. **Information, not decoration.** Every pixel should help the SRE make a decision or understand status. No decorative elements that don't carry meaning.
2. **Hierarchy through typography and space.** Use font weight, size, and whitespace to guide attention -- not color bombardment or borders.
3. **Calm urgency.** Critical incidents (P1) should feel urgent without being alarming. Use subtle color shifts and clear labels, not flashing animations.
4. **Progressive disclosure.** Show the essential information first. Details expand on interaction or scroll -- never overwhelming the initial view.
5. **Respect the context.** Dark environment, time pressure, fatigue. High contrast where it matters, muted where it doesn't. Easy on the eyes.
